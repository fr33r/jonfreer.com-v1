News story about our accomplishment on making it to Tier One Nationals.

Up to this point in my hockey life, I had worked my way up from recreational league hockey from when I was 12 or so, to AA travel hockey. I continuously pushed myself to be the best I could be, and I played as much hockey as I possibly could.

The AAA Columbus Blue Jackets is the only AAA hockey organization in Columbus. AAA hockey in Ohio is the highest intensity level that a player can reach, as well as the highest compitition. Making it to this level of play demonstrated that you had a high potential to move on to play juniors and/or college hockey.

After trying out several years and not being chosen, I entered Nationwide Arena in 2008 with ever high focus and intensity for the tryouts. These tryouts were always nerve wrecking for me because at this level of play, a lot of politics come into the picture. It was basically a given that members of the team the previous year had a pass to be on the team next year, so there were always limited spots to compete for.

After a great tryout, I beat out thirteen other goaltenders for the one spot available on the team. My hockey dreams had finally come true, and I could say that I had become one of the elite hockey players in Columbus.

Our team made our way to Tier One Nationals in Saint Louis, Missouri after winning Regionals. This was the first time in orginization history and it was incredible to have been a part of that. Our accomplishment was even honored during the National Anthem at a Blue Jackets game, with all of us lined up on the blue line.

Although, I didn't get to play in Nationals as much as I would have wanted, I still look at it as an amazing event in my life. To say that we were one of sixteen teams in the United States to compete in Nationals is truly amazing, and something few can say.

Many of the players on my team went on to be successful. Two of them have even gone on to be drafted to the NHL.

The most important take away from this experience was actually me. A bit odd to say I know, but it was incredible how far I had come in one year. My agility on the ice was honed, my sight for the puck as well as situations unfolding on the ice were sharp, and my competitiveness was at an all time high. I was the best I could be. I am still proud of that.
