In my Introduction to Algorithms course (CSE464), we were required to complete a research project on an algorithm or data structure that we had not discussed in class.

To get the creative juices flowing, my professor mandated that we produce a short write-up of anything and everything that interested us. We then met with him to discuss what algorithms or algorithm topics we could dive into that related to one of our interests.

I wrote about a few things that I thought had potential, one of which was my passion for ice hockey. My professor informed me that motion detection would be an interesting and fun topic to explore in the realm of ice hockey.

After much research, I began to develop my own motion tracker. I developed the software in Java using the Processing IDE and libraries. I then built a crude model of an ice hockey rink with a black ping pong ball acting as a puck to test and demonstrate my fully functional motion tracker.
