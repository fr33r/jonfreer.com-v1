Soon after arriving to Miami University in 2010, I took interest in the well-known Greek community. I found myself spending time with the brothers of Phi Gamma Delta (FIJI) and I was asked to attend their recruitment events.

Amidst the pressure that the chapter was receiving from the university, I chose to pledge for the fraternity. During my pledge process, I became very close with my small pledge class of fifteen people as we all dedicated a significant amount of time and effort to join one of the best fraternities Miami had to offer.

During my sophomore year, I was elected house manager for FIJI. I was in charge of any and all house repairs, fire code inspections, property appearance, and house utilities.

Nearly all of the incredible memories I have during my four years are attributed to my involvement in FIJI. I am truly proud to be a member and grateful for the friendships, memories, values, traditions, and opportunities the fraternity gave me.

In May of 2013, the fraternity house was a victim of an arson and nearly burned down. All of the brothers stored their belongings in the house as an alternative to bringing them back home for the summer. Many of my closest friends lost everything they owned and walked away with just the clothes on their back.

Despite thorough investigation, the person(s) responsible have not been brought to justice. However, reconstruction has begun, and is expected to be completed in August of 2015.

As the result of proactive actions by our elected executive board, our chapter made an agreement with the Sigma Chi fraternity to occupy their vacant house on campus until our situation was resolved.
