I attended Worthington Kilbourne High School (WKHS) from 2006 until 2010. It was here where I first began to realize who I was, how much I loved where I grew up, and made friendships that are still going strong today.

Remembering this time in my life is more difficult than I feel it is for most people. I spent the first three years being very involved in ice hockey. At some points I missed school for weeks at a time, making it difficult to really socialize and have the traditional high school experience.

However, my senior year was one for the books and I believe it was one of the best years of my life. My eyes were opened to the social life of high school, and I had the opportunity to get know the very few amount of people in the world that grew up in the same place at the same time as me.

Upon graduation in 2010, I had a difficult time deciding where to move on to college. Nearly all of my friends were heading to The Ohio State University (OSU), but I wanted to look beyond the Columbus area to see what Ohio had to offer.

It was a difficult decision, but I decided to venture two and a half hours to Oxford, Ohio and attend Miami University.
